## Relevant Industry Standards or Protocols

- **EU Pay Transparency Directive (2023/970/EU)** — Effective June 2026; requires EU employers to publish salary ranges, report gender pay gaps, and enable pay equity audits — the single biggest regulatory driver of new software purchases in this category.
- **U.S. State Pay Transparency Laws** — Colorado (2021), California (2023), New York (2023), Illinois (2025), and expanding; require salary range disclosure in job postings, driving demand for comp banding software.
- **FLSA (Fair Labor Standards Act)** — U.S. federal framework for exempt/non-exempt classification that underlies compensation structure logic.
- **ISO/TR 30406:2017** — Sustainable employability management framework, includes guidance on fair compensation practices.
- **SHRM Compensation Data Standards** — Industry body guidelines for compensation survey methodology and data collection practices.
- **ESOP / 409A Valuation Standards (IRS)** — U.S. tax regulations governing equity compensation valuation for private companies; drives demand for 409A and cap table management integrated with comp tools.
- **IFRS 2 / ASC 718** — International and U.S. accounting standards for share-based compensation; governs equity expense recognition on financial statements.
- **O*NET / ESCO Occupational Frameworks** — U.S. and EU job taxonomy standards used as foundation for comp benchmarking job families.

## AI-Native Opportunity

- **Real-time salary band automation.** Today, comp teams spend weeks manually repricing salary bands annually by cross-referencing multiple survey vendors. An AI-native system can continuously ingest live job posting data, HRIS-submitted compensation records, and survey feeds to auto-update band midpoints and flag when bands drift out of market — turning an annual project into a continuous background process.

- **Pay equity remediation modeling.** Current pay equity tools identify gaps but leave remediation planning to analysts. An AI system can model the cost of closing all statistically significant pay gaps, prioritize by impact, generate compliant EU Pay Transparency reports automatically, and simulate the budget impact of different remediation strategies — a workflow that currently requires weeks of Excel work and compensation consultant fees.

- **Offer generation and negotiation intelligence.** Recruiters currently reference static salary bands. An AI-native compensation system can generate dynamic, personalized offer recommendations that account for candidate location, competing offers, internal equity constraints, and budget remaining — with real-time approval workflows that eliminate the "let me check with HR" delay.

- **Underserved segment: SMBs without compensation expertise.** Companies under 500 employees typically cannot afford Radford surveys ($12K+) or beqom ($55K+), so they rely on Glassdoor and LinkedIn salary insights of uncertain quality. An AI-native OSS platform with curated public data ingestion, automated band generation, and pay equity scoring could serve this segment for the first time.

- **OSS differentiation via data openness and auditability.** Compensation decisions increasingly face legal scrutiny under pay transparency laws. An open-source platform where the band-setting algorithm is auditable, the data sources are documented, and the pay equity methodology can be reviewed by regulators or works councils provides compliance confidence that closed-source SaaS vendors cannot offer — particularly in EU markets where the Pay Transparency Directive (June 2026) creates new audit obligations.
